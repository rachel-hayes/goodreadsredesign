App Flow Redesign Case Study

Rachel Hayes

Goodreads

Goodreads is a mobile application that allows book lovers to discover and read more books and keep track of books they want to read. While the app serves as a convient way to find and log books, there are many areas of improvement for user interaction and usability.

App Analysis

The app has four main pages:

  • The 'Home' page is where you can browse book recommendations and see friends' reviews.
  • The 'My Books' page is where you can update book progress and see the books you want to read.
  • The 'Discover' page is where you can see book recommendations.
  • The 'Search' page is where you can find a specific book by searching for the name, title, author, or genre.

The app is known to have an outdated interface and gives generic recommendations that are not personalized enough to the user.

The original design of Goodreads also shows elements of bias that can impact user experience and engagement.

  • Problem: Goodreads heavily promotes mainstream and highly popular books on its home page and search results, making it harder for readers seeking diverse recommendations.
    • Improvement: Introduce algorithms that prioritize diverse and personalized recommendations. This can be achieved by using user preferences, past reading habits, and interactive preference quizzes to spotlight lesser-known authors and unique genres.
  • Problem:The interface is cluttered with ads and excessive sections, implicitly biases users toward sponsored content or specific categories of books. This can make users feel overwhelmed and disoriented.
    • Improvement: Streamline the home page by minimizing ads and creating a clean, visually appealing layout. Personalized dashboards that adapt to individual user preferences can reduce the influence of commercial bias.
  • Problem:Reviews and ratings from highly active users disproportionately influence the perception of books. This creates a skewed view of quality and enjoyment, potentially marginalizing books that receive fewer reviews but still resonate with specific audiences.
    • Improvement:Display a variety of reviews by prioritizing diversity in user demographics and reading habits. Include filtering options for different types of reviewers (e.g., genre specialists, verified accounts).

User Research

Design Question

How can we improve the experience of readers using the mobile app Goodreads?

I centered my redesign of Goodreads around this question to help me focus on the types of interview questions I wanted to ask my participants.

Research Questions

Below are the questions I asked my participants during their interviews. I wanted to ask them about their experience of using Goodreads to find out what they think of its features and usability. I used ChatGPT to help me come up with some questions to ask.

1.

Can you walk me through the steps you take to find and add a book to your reading list? What parts of this feels smooth or frustrating?

2.

How do you navigate between different sections of the app? What improvements could be made to make navigation easier or more intuitive?

3.

When writing or reading reviews, what does the current design help you accomplish, and where do you feel restricted?

4.

How do you feel about the process of discovering new books on Goodreads? Are there any changes that would make finding new books more engaging?

5.

What’s your experience when trying to update your reading status or share progress with your friends? Is there anything that could be simplified?

6.

How would you describe the process of organizing your bookshelves? What changes to this design flow would make it easier to manage?

7.

If you could change or add any features to Goodreads to enhance your overall reading experience, what would that be and why?

Interview Process

I interviewed 4 people in person who I already knew use Goodreads. I created a google forms of the same interview questions for other people to fill out so that I can have as much data from different users and their points of views as possible. I posted it in my Instagram and Snapchat stories so my followers could fill it out. I also decided to have ChatGPT as one of my interviewees. I conducted the interview in the same way one would a face-to-face interview; by allowing it to answer my questions one at a time and waiting for me to ask it the other questions.

Findings

I ended up gathering information from 10 people (ChatGPT is not included). A majority of the users brought up similar problems with the app. For instance, most of them said that they want an option for the “Discover” feature to promote lesser-known books instead of mainstream recommendations that it currently provides. Also, some seemed to find the navigation between sections confusing and time-consuming. They said they want a more efficient way to access key features. In addition, users thought the organizing of bookshelves to be overwhelming and frustrating and thought it would be better to have sorting options or more customization options. A few users want an easier way to share their reading progress with friends and have more interactivity. Lastly, most of the users brought up how the star rating system is limited and they would like more flexibility such as having the option to give a book a half star rating.

ChatGPT was asked the same questions, and answered almost the same. It also mentioned the problem of the recommended books mostly being popular and repetitive, and finding lesser-known books requires a lot of scrolling. ChatGPT brought up the issue of the navigation bar having too many clicks to access lists, and desires a more customizable or streamline navigation with quicker tabs to improve efficiency. Something that wasn’t mentioned before was that having an interactive social reading experience, like virtual book clubs and buddy reading. It also suggests adding a reading tracker that analyzes habits and offers personalized recommendations based on reading patterns and moods.

Below are the percentages of the participants’ responses summarized from the previous slide.

  • 75% of users expressed frustrations with cluttered homepage and navigation issues
  • 50% of users want improvements on book discovery and rating systems
  • 33% of users mentioned that the process of logging reading progress and marking books as finished should be simplified
  • 25% of users desire better social interaction features for engaging with friends or authors

Pain Points

After analyzing the interview responses, I used ChatGPT to help me identify common pain points so that I know what users find difficult or unappealing.

Users often struggle with finding less popular books. The search function is effective for popular books but can be frustrating for lesser-known titles or niche genres. The "Discover" feature doesn't fully meet users' needs, as it tends to promote mainstream or repetitive recommendations.

Users find the navigation between sections and home page layout confusing. They want quicker, more intuitive access to key features like their bookshelves, profile, and recommendations.

Managing and organizing bookshelves is not user-friendly. Users would appreciate bulk actions, easier sorting options, and more visual or interactive features for shelves.

Updating reading progress could be smoother and more engaging. Users want better visibility of their updates and an easier way to share reading progress with friends.

The current star rating system and review formatting options feel limiting. Users want more flexibility, such as half-star ratings and better ways to customize their reviews.div>

Design Requirements

I developed 5 design requirements based on these pain points to ensure that my redesign meets these requirements.

1.

The app should allow users to filter search results by multiple genres, popularity, and book format because users find it difficult to discover less popular or niche books using the current search system.

2.

The app should introduce half-star ratings and allow for more detailed review formatting options because users feel restricted in how they can rate and review books, leading to less accurate feedback.

3.

The app should provide drag-and-drop functionality for moving books between shelves and editing options because managing bookshelves is currently a cumbersome process with too many steps and little flexibility.

4.

The app should include a quick-access feature to update reading progress and allow real-time visibility of progress for friends because updating reading status and sharing it with friends feels disconnected and requires too many steps.

5.

The app should offer a customizable home page that allows users to prioritize sections like their profile, bookshelves, or recommendations because users find the current homepage cluttered and difficult to navigate, leading to frustration.

Personas

I created two personas, with Persona 1 being the primary persona and Persona 2 being the secondary persona. I used my interview findings to develop their profiles to make sure that the redesign meets a variety of needs regardless of the user.

Persona 1 - Emily Carter

Slogan: "Finding the hidden gems in the world of books!"
Key Characteristics:
Age: 20
Gender: Female
Occupation: College student (Psychology major, Business minor)
Reading Habits: Avid reader; frequently reviews books on Goodreads.
Lifestyle: Busy schedule with classes, studying, and part-time work; often reads during commutes or breaks.
Tech Savvy: Comfortable using mobile apps but prefers clean, user-friendly interfaces

Represents 70% of users

Key Goals:
Discover New Books: Wants to find unique, lesser-known books that align with her interests, avoiding mainstream and repetitive recommendations.
Manage Reading Progress: Seeks an efficient way to log her reading progress without interrupting her busy schedule.
Visual Appeal: Prefers a visually appealing home screen that is easy to navigate and free of clutter and ads.
Optimize Time: Looking for tools within the app that help her quickly find and add books, making the most of her limited reading time.

Persona 2 - Laura Wilson

Slogan: “Simplifying my reading journey, one review at a time.”
Key Characteristics:
Age: 40
Gender: Female
Occupation: Goverment contracting professional

Tech Savviness: Moderate, uses the Goodreads app mainly to log books and write reviews but seeks more simplicity and intuitive navigation.

Represents 30% of users

Easier Book Progress Tracking: Wants a simpler, more intuitive method for logging book progress on Goodreads.
Half-Star Rating System: Seeks more control over book ratings, specifically with half-star options.

I used the information gathered through user research and ideation to create an interactive mockup in Figma of the redesigned user flow of rating books. To accommadte impairments, I made sure that the colors and fonts I used stood out and contrast. In a different user flow, I made it so that the user can change the color theme to further meet their needs. Chatbot

I created a chatbot that helps users discover new book recommendations that are unique and niche to them. They would answer the questions the bot asks, and it would give them book recommendations.

A/B Testing

I decided to see if structured prompts would affect review completion and user engagement because I thought it would be interesting to see if users find these structured prompts helpful for expressing opinions or if they prefer the freedom of open-ended responses. I added a new element that allows users to answer specific prompts as compared to an open-ended review box to see if that would affect user experience and engagement.

Findings

I had 5 users interact with one flow (10 users total) and asked them follow-up questions about their experience.
Overall, screen B performed better than screen A. A majority of the users preferred having prompts to give them some starting points on writing a review.

  • Screen A Results
    • 40% of users were satisfied with the experience of the open-ended aspect of this flow.
    • 60% of users mentioned how it would be better if they were told how to write a review or given some starting points.
  • Screen B Results
    • 80% of users were satisfied with the suggested prompts of this flow because they felt it would help give them ideas on how to write a review.
    • One thing that surprised me was how a few users mentioned how having this as an option would be perfect, but they still would want the option to write their own review as well.

I used these findings to update my prototype to be the final design that was presented previously. I originally had Screen A in my mockup, but based on the findings from this test, I combined the two so that users have the option to write their own free-response or have guided prompts. I think this final design ensures that users have even more choices when it comes to reviewing books.

AI

AI Analysis

I used ChatGPT to help me perform user research for this project. I think it mostly had a positive impact regarding my experience for this project because it gave me ideas and inspiration if I was stuck on anything. For instance, it gave me good interview and follow-up questions, which helped me form my questions around that. Also, ChatGPT helped keep me on the right track to ensure that I found all of the pain points in my interview responses. I inserted my responses in ChatGPT and it summarized the results, making me work more efficiently and helped me make sure that I didn't miss any important trends in data. I did notice that it gave me some biased results, but I just ignored them or I tried to write my prompt in a different way to see if it would give me a different answer.
